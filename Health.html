DIPLOMA

A Diploma in Polytechnic is a three-year program that provides practical skills in fields like Mechanical, Civil, and Computer Science Engineering. It focuses on hands-on training, internships, and project work to prepare students for industry roles. Graduates can work as Junior Engineers or Technicians or pursue higher education through lateral entry into engineering degrees. The course bridges the gap between academics and real-world applications. It is an affordable and career-focused option for technical education.

ITI

An ITI (Industrial Training Institute) course is a vocational program for students after 10th grade, offering practical training in trades like Electrician, Fitter, and Mechanic. These courses, lasting 6 months to 2 years, prepare students for entry-level industrial jobs or self-employment. ITI is ideal for gaining job-ready skills or pursuing further technical education like a diploma in engineering.

INTERMEDIATE

An Intermediate course after 10th grade is a two-year program that prepares students for higher education. It offers streams like Science, Commerce, and Arts, based on career goals. Science focuses on subjects like Physics, Chemistry, and Math or Biology, while Commerce emphasizes accounting and economics. Arts includes subjects like history and literature. This course lays a foundation for professional and undergraduate studies.

FINE ARTS

A Fine Arts course after the 10th grade is designed for students passionate about creative fields like painting, sculpture, graphic design, or applied arts. It focuses on developing artistic skills, creativity, and an understanding of visual aesthetics. The course includes practical training in various mediums and techniques, along with theoretical knowledge of art history and culture. Fine Arts courses can lead to careers in fields such as design, animation, illustration, or teaching, offering a foundation for further specialization in creative industries.
